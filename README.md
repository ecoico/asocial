# Asocial

**Being ordinary is the value, not the exception.**

Asocial is a social web app designed as the antithesis of Instagram and performative social media. It celebrates the mundane, the banal, and the unremarkable moments of everyday life.

## Philosophy

Modern social media has created a culture of curation, comparison, and performance. Every post is optimized for engagement, every photo filtered to perfection, every moment crafted to project success.

Asocial rejects this entirely.

Here, the goal is to share the ordinary:
- Your coffee getting cold
- Waiting for a late bus
- Folding mismatched socks
- Staring at your ceiling
- The weather being "fine"

No ostentation. No competition. No success metrics. Just... existence.

## Design Principles

### Visual Aesthetic
- **Pastel color palette**: Soft, muted tones that don't demand attention
- **Ample whitespace**: Breathing room instead of visual noise
- **Clean typography**: Readable Inter font, nothing flashy
- **Subtle animations**: Gentle fades, not jarring transitions
- **No gradients or neon**: Flat, honest design

### User Experience
- **Anti-aspirational placeholders**: "What uninteresting thing are you doing?"
- **No gamification**: No streaks, badges, or achievement systems
- **Quiet interface**: No bright red notifications or urgent calls-to-action
- **Deliberate friction**: No infinite scroll (future consideration)

## Philosophy in Practice

### Why no likes?
Because your worth isn't measured in hearts. A post about cold coffee is just as valid as any other post.

### Why chronological feed?
Algorithms promote "engaging" content, which encourages performative posting. Chronological order treats all moments equally.

### Why no follower counts?
Social hierarchy creates pressure to perform. Here, everyone's voice has equal weight.

### Why such muted colors?
Bright, saturated colors demand attention and create urgency. Pastels are calm, neutral, and don't compete for your dopamine.

### Why no infinite scroll?
(Future consideration) Deliberate endpoints encourage intentional usage, not mindless consumption.

## Customization

Feel free to modify:
- **Color palette** - Update CSS variables in `styles.css`
- **Character limits** - Change max lengths in HTML and JS
- **Mock data** - Edit `js/data.js` to add your own mundane posts
- **User profile** - Change `CURRENT_USER` in `js/data.js`

## Future Considerations

Potential features that maintain the anti-aspirational philosophy:
- Random posting times to discourage "optimal" posting strategies
- Auto-delete after 30 days to embrace impermanence
- "Boring score" that awards less exciting posts
- Deliberately slow loading to discourage addiction
